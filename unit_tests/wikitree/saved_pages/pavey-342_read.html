Biography

Harry was born on 18 July 1852 in Plymouth, Devon, England. His parents were William Henry Pavey and Elizabeth (Ellacott) Pavey. Harry was the 12th and final child of the couple.[1]

Harry was baptised on 23 March 1855 in Plymouth St Andrew. He was baptised at the same time as his four closest older siblings. It appears that his parents had their 5 youngest children baptised shortly before they moved to London. The family was living on Athenaeum Street at the time of the baptism.[2]

In the 1861 census Harry was living at 16 Queen's Crescent, Kentish Town, St Pancras, Middlesex with his father and older siblings Charles and Fanny. His mother was staying with their daughter Marianne (who had just given birth) and her family.[3][4]

Harry married Amelia Elizabeth Littlemore in October 1870 in Pancras, London.[5][6] They had 10 children. Their first three sons died in infancy. Five sons and two daughters survived to adulthood. (Note: according to the 1911 census there was one more child who died in childhood but no birth registration for that child has been found).

In the 1871 census Harry was living at 65 Prince of Wales Rd, St Pancras, London with his wife and one month old son. They were living in the same house as his parents but considered a separate household. Harry was a blind maker like his father.[7][8]

In the 1881 census Harry was living at 367 Kentish Town Road, St Pancras, London with his wife and three children. He was a Picture Frame Maker.[9][10]

Harry Alfred Pavey with his wife and children Mary Elizabeth "Maisie", Harry Alfred and Amelia Annie. Possibly taken by his brother-in-law in 1881 (his brother Frederick married Eliza Hatt, they were a family of photographers).

In the 1891 census Harry was living at 318 Kentish Town Road, St Pancras, London, England. He was with his wife and six children and was working as a Picture Frame Maker.[11][12][13]

On 14 April 1894 a bankruptcy notice appeared in the Morning Post: "H. Pavey, Kentish-town-road. St. Pancras, picture frame maker and music seller, April 23rd at 2:30."[14]

On 3 December 1900 Harry was briefly admitted to St Pancras Workhouse but appears to have been immediately discharged at his own request ("OR"). His occupation of "Picture-frame Maker" was recorded.[15]

In the 1901 census Harry was living in St Pancras, London, England. Relation to Head: Head.[16][17]

In 1902 Harry was in the City Directory at 318 Kentish Town Road listed under "Carvers and Gilders", he was probably still a picture-frame maker.[18]

In the 1911 census Harry was living at 110 Highgate Hill, Upper Holloway with his wife and two of his sons.[19][20]

In 1914 according to an electoral register Harry was still living at 110 Highgate Hill.[21]

Harry died on 5 January 1914 (date from headstone). He was probably still living at 110 Highgate Hill. The death was registered in the Hampstead registration district.[22]

Harry was buried on 9 January 1914 in the same family plot as his parents in Plot 1, Camden Area in St. Pancras and Islington Cemetery (this is actually in East Finchley, about 5 miles from where they lived).[23]

Gravestone of William Henry Pavey and family

Research Notes

There is a newspaper article about a comic singer called Harry Pavey in 1898. This is probably a different Harry Pavey but some of his family were musical so it is possible. The address in the notice is "59 Alderney St, Pimlico, SW" which does not seem a match.[24]
